# Executive Summary

The system now has two clearly different layers:

## 1) Shortlist
A customer-facing decision intelligence product focused on helping buyers:
- create a shortlist
- compare vendors
- evaluate options with collaborators
- identify risks, dealbreakers, and fine-print issues
- make better purchase decisions with evidence

## 2) Decision Intelligence Console
An internal operating system used to:
- discover opportunities
- run research and evaluation workflows
- manage agent systems
- operate shared infrastructure
- build and launch niche applications
- monitor live systems and experiments
- support marketing, design, and GTM operations

## Why the separation matters
If the console and Shortlist are treated as the same thing, the product boundary gets blurry.

Shortlist needs a crisp market story.

The console can stay broader, more powerful, and more experimental because it is internal.

## Strategic posture
Build the console as the engine behind multiple outcomes:
- better product decisions
- better company creation
- better research velocity
- better experiments
- better app operations
- better commercialization of future tools

## Practical implication
The internal console should track:
- sources and APIs
- opportunities and undersupplied markets
- experiments and results
- prompts, agents, tools, and workflows
- active applications and their health
- content, launches, and messaging systems

That gives the team a true command center, while Shortlist stays the focused commercial wedge.
