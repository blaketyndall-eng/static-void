# Forward Moves — Strategic Sequence

This document captures the three forward moves that align strategy, execution, and capability-building across the Decision Intelligence Console and product portfolio.

## Move 1 — Make the Console the Operating Core

**Goal:** Use the Console as the single control plane for product decisions, experiment orchestration, and intelligence ops.

### Outcomes
- One shared mission-control workflow for opportunities, experiments, decisions, and launches.
- Common event/telemetry model across internal and external products.
- Faster cycle time from signal → decision → action.

### Build Focus
- Mission control and activity log hardening.
- Source registry + provenance discipline.
- Decision workflow templates and reusable operating rituals.

---

## Move 2 — Productize Shortlist as the Commercial Wedge

**Goal:** Ship and iterate Shortlist as the external revenue engine while feeding insight back into the Console.

### Outcomes
- Repeatable customer-facing decision workflow.
- Better compare/evidence/dealbreaker intelligence.
- Clear operating loop between customer outcomes and internal roadmap.

### Build Focus
- Decision workflow UX and trust surfaces.
- Fine-print and risk extraction quality.
- Collaboration and sharing for teams.

---

## Move 3 — Stand Up the Software Arm

**Goal:** Build an execution arm that can repeatedly design, build, debug, and launch software products with increasing autonomy.

### Outcomes
- Distinct capability tracks for coding, debugging, and design.
- Defined maturation path for operators and agents.
- Repeatable 30/60/90 cadence for execution.

### Build Focus
- Capability matrix and competency milestones.
- Playbooks, templates, and QA standards.
- Continuous improvement loop grounded in shipped work.

---

## Why this sequence

1. **Core first** (Console) ensures decisions improve over time.
2. **Wedge second** (Shortlist) creates market pressure and signal quality.
3. **Execution flywheel third** (Software Arm) compounds delivery velocity and quality.
