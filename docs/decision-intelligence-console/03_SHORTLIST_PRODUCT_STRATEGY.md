# Shortlist Product Strategy

## Product boundary
Shortlist is the external product customers buy.

It is not the internal console.

That distinction matters because it keeps the sellable wedge crisp while allowing the console to stay broader and more experimental.

## Core job to be done
Help buyers and teams make better purchase decisions by:
- finding options worth considering
- structuring comparisons
- surfacing dealbreakers
- organizing evaluation evidence
- reducing regret and uncertainty

## Best early wedge
A workflow-centered decision product for software/vendor evaluation.

Especially useful for:
- procurement-adjacent teams
- business buyers
- IT/security stakeholders
- cross-functional evaluation groups

## High-value product elements
- compare-my-shortlist
- evidence pack
- regret radar
- fine print scanner / dealbreaker detection
- friction score
- decision brief
- collaborator evaluation workflow
- decision memory

## Why it can win
Shortlist does not need to solve every decision problem first.

It needs to own one painful, recurring workflow and become the best structured system for it.

## Strategic role relative to the console
The console should make Shortlist better by powering:
- intelligence gathering
- recommendation logic
- workflow experimentation
- source quality improvements
- agent-assisted summarization
- pricing/GTM testing

So the console is the internal engine.
Shortlist is the customer-facing outcome.
